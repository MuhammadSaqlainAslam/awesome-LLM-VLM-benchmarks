## Problem
With the rapid influx of new benchmarks, there is no standardized meta-evaluation to determine which benchmarks are actually reliable, high-quality, or resistant to model over-fitting.

## Method
The authors introduce **Benchmark²**, a meta-evaluation framework that uses Cross-Benchmark Ranking Consistency (CBRC) and Discriminability Scores to filter out "noisy" or "saturated" test instances.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **Meta-Benchmark:** Benchmark²
- **Evaluated Sets:** 15 major benchmarks (MMLU, GSM8K, HumanEval, etc.)
- **Models:** 11 frontier LLMs/VLMs

## Key Results
The study found that removing the bottom 30% of "low-discriminability" questions across 15 benchmarks actually *increased* model ranking stability, achieving a consistency score of **0.93 Spearman’s rho**.

## FoxBrain Relevance
Highly relevant for our roadmap. We can use the Benchmark² methodology to prune our internal evaluation suites, ensuring we only test FoxBrain against high-signal, high-difficulty questions.
