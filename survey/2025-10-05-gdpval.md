# GDPval: Evaluating AI Model Performance on Real-World Economically Valuable Tasks (2025)

## Problem
Existing benchmarks do not measure whether AI models can perform tasks of genuine economic value in actual professional workflows. High scores on academic reasoning benchmarks do not predict whether a model can complete the kind of work that professionals do every day and that contributes meaningfully to GDP. GDPval fills this gap by evaluating AI directly on real tasks from the largest sectors of the U.S. economy.

## Method
Tasks were derived by professional contributors with an average of **14 years of experience** across 44 occupations in 9 top GDP-contributing U.S. sectors. Evaluation uses **blind human expert pairwise comparisons** (three experts per task, nine comparisons per task). An experimental automated grader (66% agreement with human raters; human inter-rater agreement 71%) is available for the open-source gold subset of 220 tasks. 67.7% of tasks require interaction with at least one reference file (spreadsheets, documents, presentations, images, audio, video, CAD). An independent Artificial Analysis evaluation (GDPval-AA) applies agentic scaffolding with shell access and web browsing, deriving ELO ratings from blind pairwise comparisons. Published by **OpenAI** (arXiv: 2510.04374, October 2025).

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **Full set:** 1,320 tasks across 44 occupations
- **Open-source gold subset:** 220 tasks (publicly available)
- **9 sectors (by U.S. GDP share):** Real Estate & Rental/Leasing (13.8%), Government (11.3%), Manufacturing (10.0%), Professional/Scientific/Technical Services (8.1%), Health Care & Social Assistance (7.6%), Finance & Insurance (7.4%), Retail Trade (6.3%), Wholesale Trade (5.8%), Information (5.4%)
- **Total annual earnings represented:** ~$3 trillion
- **File-interaction tasks:** 67.7% of all tasks require working with reference files

## Key Results

**OpenAI evaluation (human pairwise win rate, gold subset):**

| Model | Win Rate |
|---|---|
| Claude Opus 4.1 | **47.6%** |
| GPT-5 | 39.0% |
| o3 | 35.2% |
| o4-mini | 29.1% |
| GPT-4o | 12.5% |

**Artificial Analysis GDPval-AA leaderboard (ELO, agentic scaffold):**

| Model | ELO |
|---|---|
| GPT-5.4 (xhigh) | **1,676** |
| Claude Sonnet 4.6 (Adaptive Reasoning) | 1,648 |
| Claude Opus 4.6 (Adaptive Reasoning) | 1,610 |

Key finding: "Models are beginning to approach parity with industry experts." Frontier model performance is improving roughly linearly over time — making this benchmark a direct proxy for economic value of AI deployment.

## FoxBrain Relevance
GDPval's Manufacturing sector tasks (10.0% of benchmark, contributing $3T GDP) are the most directly relevant to Foxconn's operations. The benchmark explicitly includes tasks requiring file interaction with spreadsheets, CAD files, and technical documents — the exact modalities FoxBrain encounters on the factory floor. The win rate metric (models approaching parity with 14-year professionals) provides FoxBrain's product roadmap with a concrete production readiness threshold: target >40% win rate against Foxconn manufacturing domain experts before agentic deployment.
