# KDR-Bench: Knowledgeable Deep Research Benchmark (2026)

## Problem
"Deep research" agents — systems that autonomously gather, synthesise, and report on complex topics — have emerged as a high-value LLM application. But existing evaluations use web-only research tasks where information is available in plain text. Real-world knowledge work requires integrating *structured knowledge* (database tables, spreadsheets, structured reports) with *unstructured documents* (PDFs, web pages, academic papers) and synthesising findings into comprehensive multimodal outputs. No benchmark tested deep research agents on tasks requiring heterogeneous knowledge source integration, leaving a critical blind spot for enterprise research automation.

## Method
**KDR-Bench** (arXiv: 2604.07720, April 9, 2026) constructs **41 expert-level questions** across **9 domains** that require genuine integration of structured + unstructured knowledge sources, with **1,252+ structured knowledge resources (tables)** embedded in the evaluation corpus. Questions are designed so they cannot be answered from unstructured text alone — correct answers require discovering, querying, and synthesising relevant table data alongside document evidence.

**Three metric categories** aligned to the distinct challenges of knowledge-intensive deep research:
1. **General-purpose metrics** — overall report quality, factual accuracy, citation coverage
2. **Knowledge-centric metrics** — accuracy of structured data retrieval, correct numerical reasoning over tables, cross-source synthesis quality
3. **Vision-enhanced metrics** — quality of charts, tables, and figures generated in the output report

The benchmark also evaluates the authors' proposed **HKA (Hybrid Knowledge Agent) framework**, which augments standard retrieval with structured knowledge graph querying.

Authors: Wenxuan Liu, Zixuan Li, Long Bai, Chunmao Zhang, Fenghui Zhang, Zhuo Chen et al. (16 total).

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **41 expert-level questions** across 9 knowledge domains
- **1,252+ structured knowledge resources** (tables) in the evaluation corpus
- **3 metric categories:** General-purpose, Knowledge-centric, Vision-enhanced
- **Baseline comparison:** Standard RAG agents vs. Gemini Deep Research vs. HKA framework
- **Metric focus:** Structured data retrieval accuracy, cross-source synthesis, report visual quality

## Key Results

| System | General Metrics | Knowledge-Centric | Vision-Enhanced | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **HKA Framework** | Competitive | **Best** | **Best** | Authors' proposed system |
| Gemini Deep Research | Strong | Weaker | Weaker | Best existing commercial agent |
| Standard RAG | Baseline | Low | Low | Fails on structured data |

- **HKA framework surpasses Gemini Deep Research on knowledge-centric and vision-enhanced metrics** — structured knowledge retrieval is the key differentiator
- **Standard RAG pipelines fail significantly on knowledge-centric tasks** — text-only retrieval cannot answer questions requiring table data synthesis
- All systems show the largest performance gap on questions requiring synthesis across 5+ heterogeneous sources (multiple tables + multiple documents simultaneously)
- **Vision-enhanced output quality** (charts, tables in final reports) differentiates agents more than text quality at expert-level tasks

## FoxBrain Relevance
KDR-Bench directly benchmarks the deep research capabilities required for Foxconn's R&D intelligence workflows: competitive analysis reports, technology landscape assessments, supply chain risk analyses, and regulatory compliance summaries all require synthesising structured (market databases, financial tables, spec sheets) with unstructured (research papers, regulatory filings, news) sources. The benchmark's finding that standard RAG fails on structured data integration is a critical design constraint for FoxBrain's research agent: the pipeline must include structured knowledge graph querying alongside document retrieval. The 3-category evaluation framework (general + knowledge-centric + vision-enhanced) provides a ready-made scoring rubric for FoxBrain's internal research report quality assessment.
