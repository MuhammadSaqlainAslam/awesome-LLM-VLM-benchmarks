# Uni-MMMU: A Massive Multi-discipline Multimodal Unified Benchmark (2025)

## Problem
Existing multimodal benchmarks evaluate visual understanding and visual generation in isolation, leaving the **bidirectional synergy** between these two capabilities unmeasured. No benchmark forced models to simultaneously leverage understanding to guide generation *and* generation as a cognitive scaffold for reasoning.

## Method
Uni-MMMU defines **eight tasks** grouped into two paradigms:
1. **Generation aids understanding:** Maze navigation, Sliding puzzle, Geometry diagram reconstruction, Jigsaw assembly — models must generate intermediate visual outputs to reason over them.
2. **Understanding guides generation:** Science (Physics, Chemistry, Biology) and Code Rendering — models must comprehend domain concepts to synthesize correct visual outputs.

Each task includes **verifiable intermediate reasoning steps**, unique ground truths, and a reproducible scoring protocol covering both textual and visual outputs. The benchmark enables equitable comparison across unified models, generation-only models, and understanding-only models.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **Uni-MMMU Suite:** 8 bidirectionally coupled tasks spanning science, mathematics, coding, and puzzles.
- **Modalities:** Text, images, diagrams, code renderings.
- **Metrics:** Task-specific accuracy for textual answers + visual output correctness scores.

## Key Results
Extensive evaluation of SOTA unified, generation-only, and understanding-only models reveals **substantial performance disparities** and strong cross-modal dependencies. Models that excel at understanding alone cannot match unified models on generation-aided reasoning tasks, and vice versa. The benchmark exposes when and how visual generation and understanding reinforce one another.

## FoxBrain Relevance
Uni-MMMU directly challenges FoxBrain's multimodal pipeline: if FoxBrain is expected to generate engineering diagrams, CAD sketches, or process charts *while* reasoning over them, this benchmark is the stress test. It reveals whether FoxBrain's vision encoder and generation head are truly coupled or just independently capable.
