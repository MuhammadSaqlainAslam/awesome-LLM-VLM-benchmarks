# EMMA: An Enhanced MultiModal ReAsoning Benchmark (2025)

## Problem
Existing MLLM benchmarks over-emphasize text-heavy tasks or shallow visual understanding, and fail to test genuine cross-modal reasoning — tasks where visual and textual information must be jointly reasoned rather than processed independently. Models can achieve high scores by solving problems in just one modality, masking fundamental reasoning deficiencies. EMMA is specifically constructed to require "organic multimodal reasoning" where neither modality alone is sufficient.

## Method
EMMA contains 2,788 problems (1,796 newly constructed) across four domains: **Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, and Coding**. Every problem explicitly requires joint visual-textual reasoning — no problem can be solved by ignoring either modality. An **EMMA-mini** subset of 400 questions (100 per domain) enables fast evaluation. The benchmark evaluates Chain-of-Thought prompting and test-time compute scaling strategies across both proprietary (GPT-4o, o3, o4-mini, Gemini-2.5-pro-exp) and open-source (VL-Rethinker-72B) frontier models. Error analysis categorizes failure modes with particular attention to whether errors originate in visual or textual reasoning steps. Accepted at **ICML 2025 as an Oral presentation**.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **EMMA full:** 2,788 problems across Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Coding
- **EMMA-mini:** 400 problems (100 per domain) for fast evaluation
- **Evaluated models:** GPT-4o, o3, o4-mini, Gemini-2.5-pro-exp-03-25, VL-Rethinker-72B, and others
- **Human baseline** established per domain

## Key Results
- Best model + best prompting strategy still trails humans by **27%** on EMMA
- Even with Chain-of-Thought, frontier models "show significant limitations in handling complex multimodal and multi-step reasoning tasks"
- Top performers on EMMA-mini: Gemini-2.5-pro-exp-03-25 and o4-mini (proprietary); VL-Rethinker-72B (open-source)
- Error analysis reveals the **majority of failures arise from visual reasoning steps**, not language reasoning — confirming vision is the bottleneck
- A 27% human-model gap persists even with advanced reasoning techniques (chain-of-thought, test-time scaling)

## FoxBrain Relevance
EMMA's four-domain structure — math, physics, chemistry, and coding — maps directly onto Foxconn's R&D and engineering workflows where visual diagrams (circuit schematics, material phase charts, process flowcharts) must be reasoned about jointly with quantitative specifications. The 27% gap to human performance flags that FoxBrain cannot yet be trusted for unsupervised multimodal STEM reasoning in high-stakes manufacturing contexts. FoxBrain should be evaluated on EMMA's Chemistry and Physics subsets before deployment in materials science or process engineering settings.
