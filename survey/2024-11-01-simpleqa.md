# SimpleQA: Measuring Short-Form Factuality in Large Language Models (2024)

## Problem
LLMs frequently hallucinate facts, yet prior factuality benchmarks were either too easy for frontier models or allowed hedged/"not sure" answers that obscure true accuracy. There was no single, clean metric for short-form parametric factual recall across diverse topics.

## Method
**SimpleQA** is a benchmark of **4,326 short, fact-seeking questions** with single, indisputable answers. Questions were created by AI trainers browsing the web, with each requiring a verifiable, time-stable answer. Questions had to induce hallucinations from either GPT-4o or GPT-3.5 during data collection to ensure sufficient difficulty. Answers are graded as **Correct / Incorrect / Not Attempted** via an LLM judge, yielding an F1-style accuracy score.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
* **SimpleQA** — 4,326 questions spanning science, history, geography, pop culture, and more.
* **SimpleQA Verified** (arXiv:2509.07968) — a refined 1,000-prompt subset with de-duplication, topic balancing, and source reconciliation for even higher label reliability.
* **Metrics:** Correct %, Not Attempted %, and combined **F1-style score**.

## Key Results
* **GPT-4o** achieves ~38.2% correct on original SimpleQA — frontier models still hallucinate frequently.
* **SimpleQA Verified:** Gemini 2.5 Pro reaches **55.6 F1**, current SOTA.
* Even top commercial models remain far below human accuracy, confirming the benchmark's difficulty ceiling.

## FoxBrain Relevance
SimpleQA is the go-to **hallucination stress-test** for factual grounding. Run it on every FoxBrain checkpoint before production. The "Not Attempted" rate is a key signal — a model that says "I don't know" correctly is preferable to one that confidently fabricates.
