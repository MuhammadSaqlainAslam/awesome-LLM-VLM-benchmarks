# TEMPER: Testing Emotional Perturbation Effects on LLM Reasoning (2026)

## Problem
LLMs are deployed in emotionally charged contexts — customer service, mental health support, crisis response, conflict de-escalation — where user messages often contain high-affect language alongside quantitative or logical requests. Prior work showed models can be manipulated by irrelevant emotional framing, but no systematic benchmark measured the *magnitude* and *universality* of this degradation across diverse model families and scales, or whether the effect could be neutralised. The absence of standardised emotional-robustness testing means no organisation deploying LLMs in human-facing roles can reliably quantify their emotional vulnerability.

## Method
**TEMPER** (Temper-5400, arXiv: 2604.07801, April 9, 2026) constructs **5,400 semantically equivalent problem pairs** — each pair consisting of an identical reasoning problem presented once with neutral framing and once with emotionally charged framing. Emotional variants preserve all numerical content and logical structure; only surrounding affective language changes (frustration, urgency, distress, enthusiasm). Source problems drawn from GSM8K, MultiArith, and ARC-Challenge to cover arithmetic, multi-step reasoning, and science QA. A neutralisation condition — preprocessing emotional inputs to remove affective language before model inference — tests recoverability.

**18 models evaluated** spanning 1B to frontier scale across four model families.

Authors: Atahan Dokme, Benjamin Reichman, Larry Heck.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **5,400 problem pairs:** neutral vs. emotionally framed (semantically identical content)
- **Sources:** GSM8K, MultiArith, ARC-Challenge
- **Emotion types:** Frustration, urgency, distress, enthusiasm
- **18 models tested** (1B → frontier scale, 4 model families)
- **Conditions:** Neutral, Emotional, Neutralised (emotional → preprocessed → model)
- **Metric:** Accuracy delta (Emotional − Neutral); recovery rate under neutralisation

## Key Results

| Condition | Accuracy Effect |
|---|---|
| Neutral baseline | Reference |
| Emotional framing | **−2 to −10 pp** across all 18 models |
| After neutralisation | Recovers most lost performance |

- **Emotional framing reduces accuracy by 2–10 percentage points across all 18 tested models** — no model is immune regardless of size or architecture
- **Neutralisation (preprocessing to remove affective language) recovers most lost performance**, confirming the degradation is in the input framing, not the underlying reasoning capability
- Effect is not monotonic with model size: some larger models show *larger* drops than smaller ones on specific emotion types
- Urgency and distress framings cause larger accuracy drops than enthusiasm framings
- ARC-Challenge (science reasoning) shows larger drops than arithmetic tasks, suggesting emotional framing most harms complex multi-step reasoning

## FoxBrain Relevance
TEMPER directly quantifies the risk of deploying FoxBrain in emotionally charged manufacturing or customer-facing contexts — equipment failure reports, quality complaint escalations, supply chain crisis communications. A 2–10 pp accuracy drop from emotional framing in a system making consequential operational decisions (reject/accept lot, escalate defect, approve order) is unacceptable for production deployments. Recommended: implement TEMPER's neutralisation preprocessing as a mandatory guard layer in FoxBrain's input pipeline for any human-facing or crisis-context deployment. Also run TEMPER's full 5,400-pair evaluation on FoxBrain checkpoints before release to quantify the exact vulnerability profile for each emotion type.
