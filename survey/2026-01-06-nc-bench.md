# Paper Overview: NC-Bench: Evaluating Conversational Competence

## Problem
Most chat benchmarks evaluate *what* a model says (content) rather than *how* it manages a conversation (social structure). Natural conversation requires turn-taking, "repairing" misunderstandings, and closing pairs properly.

## Method
Grounded in the **IBM Natural Conversation Framework (NCF)**. It operationalizes "conversation competence" through 120 generic patterns. It tests sequence management: how a model handles "Got it," "Never mind," or multi-turn repair requests.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
* **Key Finding:** Models are good at answering inquiries but **struggle with repair tasks** (especially "repeat/redo" requests) and closing sequences.

## FoxBrain Relevance
This benchmark will refine the **FoxBrain Voice Interface**. By focusing on "NC-Bench" metrics, we can make our assistant feel more human-like in its interaction flow, reducing "robotic" or repetitive conversational loops.
