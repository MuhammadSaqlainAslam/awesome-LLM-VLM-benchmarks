# OMNI-MATH: A Universal Olympiad-Level Mathematics Benchmark for LLMs (2024)

## Problem
Benchmarks like GSM8K and MATH are effectively saturated — OpenAI o1 achieves 94.8% on MATH — making them useless for differentiating frontier models. There was no comprehensive, hierarchically categorised, Olympiad-level benchmark covering the full breadth of competition mathematics with rigorous difficulty stratification across 10+ levels.

## Method
4,428 competition-level problems were collected from the world's top mathematics competitions: **IMO, Putnam, USAMO, China National Olympiad (CNO), APMO, HMMT, and AoPS online repositories**. Each problem was human-annotated with 97.3% verified accuracy. Problems are organised into a hierarchical taxonomy of **33+ sub-domains** across 7 top-level areas (Algebra, Pre-Calculus, Calculus, Geometry, Discrete Mathematics, Number Theory, Applied Mathematics) and **10+ difficulty levels** corresponding to competition tiers T0–T4 (IMO/Putnam at the apex, down to introductory competitions). Published at **ICLR 2025**.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **4,428 competition-level problems** (largest Olympiad math benchmark at time of release)
- **33+ sub-domains**, 7 top-level mathematical areas
- **10+ difficulty levels** across 5 competition tiers (T0–T4: IMO/Putnam → USAMO/CNO → APMO/USAJMO → HMMT → introductory)
- 97.3% annotation accuracy verified by human experts
- Sources: IMO, Putnam, USAMO, CNO, APMO, HMMT, AoPS

## Key Results

| Model | Accuracy |
|---|---|
| OpenAI o1-mini | **60.54%** |
| OpenAI o1-preview | 52.55% |
| Qwen2.5-MATH-72B-Instruct | 36.20% |
| GPT-4o | 30.49% |
| QwQ-32B (rule-based eval) | 49.6% |

Even the strongest model (o1-mini) reaches only **60.54%**, leaving substantial headroom. The hierarchical difficulty structure enables fine-grained diagnosis of where individual models break down across competition tiers.

## FoxBrain Relevance
OMNI-MATH's 33+ sub-domain taxonomy enables targeted evaluation of FoxBrain's mathematical reasoning across specific sub-fields — allowing FoxBrain to be benchmarked specifically on Number Theory and Combinatorics (relevant to combinatorial optimisation in scheduling), or Geometry (relevant to spatial reasoning in manufacturing layouts). The 10+ difficulty tiers provide a fine-grained roadmap for FoxBrain's mathematical capability development, from practical engineering calculation (T4) through rigorous proof-level reasoning (T0).
