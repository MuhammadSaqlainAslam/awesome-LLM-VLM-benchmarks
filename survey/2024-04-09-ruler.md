# RULER: What's the Real Context Size of Your Long-Context Language Models? (2024)

## Problem
The widely-used Needle-in-a-Haystack (NIAH) test is an overly superficial measure of long-context ability. Models claiming to support 32K–128K token context windows were achieving near-perfect NIAH scores, yet failing on realistic tasks requiring aggregation, multi-hop reasoning, or extraction of multiple values across long documents. The real effective context length of LLMs was unknown and systematically overestimated.

## Method
RULER is a **synthetic, configurable benchmark** from NVIDIA with programmable sequence lengths and task complexity. It introduces 4 task categories covering 13 tasks total, designed so that context length and difficulty can be independently controlled. Models are evaluated at 6 context lengths: **4K, 8K, 16K, 32K, 64K, and 128K tokens**. Unlike NIAH, tasks require multi-value retrieval, variable tracking, and open-book QA with long contexts — tasks that cannot be shortcutted by simple pattern matching. Published at **COLM 2024**.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **13 tasks across 4 categories:**
  1. **Retrieval (4 tasks):** Single-NIAH, Multi-Keys NIAH, Multi-Values NIAH, Multi-Queries NIAH
  2. **Multi-hop Tracing (1 task):** Variable Tracking (VT) — follow chains of variable assignments
  3. **Aggregation (2 tasks):** Common Words Extraction (CWE), Frequent Words Extraction (FWE)
  4. **Question Answering (6 tasks):** SQuAD-based and HotpotQA-based variants at varying complexity
- **6 context lengths tested:** 4K / 8K / 16K / 32K / 64K / 128K tokens
- **17 long-context models evaluated**

## Key Results (Average Score Across All 13 Tasks)

| Model | 4K | 8K | 16K | 32K | 64K | 128K |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **GPT-4** | **96.6** | **96.3** | **95.2** | **93.2** | **87.0** | **81.2** |
| Command-R | 93.8 | 93.3 | 92.4 | 89.5 | 84.9 | 76.0 |
| Yi-34B | 93.3 | 92.2 | 91.3 | 87.5 | 83.2 | 77.3 |
| Mixtral-8x7B | 94.9 | 92.1 | 92.5 | 85.9 | 72.4 | 44.5 |
| Mistral-7B | 93.6 | 91.2 | 87.2 | 75.4 | 49.0 | 13.8 |
| ChatGLM | 87.8 | 83.4 | 78.6 | 69.9 | 56.0 | 42.0 |

Key finding: despite near-perfect NIAH scores, **almost all models degrade sharply** at longer contexts — especially on aggregation and multi-hop tasks. Of models claiming 32K+ context, only half maintained satisfactory performance at 32K. Effective context length is systematically shorter than claimed advertised length.

## FoxBrain Relevance
RULER directly addresses FoxBrain's core deployment risk in long-document workflows (technical manuals, multi-page inspection reports, regulatory compliance documents). The aggregation tasks (CWE, FWE) are especially relevant for summarisation of lengthy Foxconn manufacturing process documents; multi-hop tracing (VT) maps to dependency chain analysis in supply chain or BOM reasoning. FoxBrain should be evaluated on RULER at the 32K and 64K tiers before deploying on any document that exceeds standard context — degradation on aggregation tasks at long contexts is the most dangerous silent failure mode in production RAG pipelines.
