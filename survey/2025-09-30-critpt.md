# CritPt: Probing the Critical Point of AI Reasoning with Frontier Physics Research (2025)

## Problem
LLM benchmarks based on textbook problems and known results cannot reveal whether models can do genuine frontier scientific research. Models that ace standard physics benchmarks may be pattern-matching against memorised solutions — not performing the novel derivations, numerical computations, and multi-step reasoning that actual research demands. CritPt probes the "critical point": the transition from superficial pattern-matching to genuine research-level physics reasoning on **unpublished, novel problems** drawn from active researchers' own work.

## Method
**50+ active physics researchers** from 30+ leading institutions each contributed problems based on their own ongoing — not yet published — research. Problems are structured as **Challenges** (full entry-level research projects) and **Checkpoints** (2–4 decomposed subtasks per challenge), enabling both end-to-end and step-level evaluation. Two evaluation setups: **(1) Self-carryover** — model uses its own prior outputs; **(2) Oracle carryover** — model receives correct prior answers, isolating error propagation. Answers use "guess-resistant" formats — floating-point arrays, symbolic expressions, Python functions — eliminating multiple-choice shortcutting. Contributors span Argonne National Laboratory, UIUC, Princeton, Caltech, Perimeter Institute, CMU, and 25+ additional institutions. Published September 2025 (arXiv: 2509.26574).

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **71 composite research challenges → 190 checkpoint subtasks**
- **12 physics domains:** Condensed Matter Physics (35.2%), Quantum Information/Science/Technology (23.9%), Atomic/Molecular/Optical Physics (19.7%), High Energy Physics (14.1%), Mathematical Physics (14.1%), Gravitation/Cosmology/Astrophysics (12.7%), Statistical Physics & Thermodynamics (12.7%), Nuclear Physics (9.9%), Nonlinear Dynamics (5.6%), Fluid Dynamics (2.8%), Biophysics (2.8%), and Interdisciplinary (46.5% of challenges span 2+ fields)
- All problems from active unpublished research — zero contamination risk

## Key Results (Challenge Accuracy / Checkpoint Accuracy)

**At publication (base models, no agentic scaffold):**

| Model | Challenge Acc. | Checkpoint Acc. |
|---|---|---|
| GPT-5 high + code & web | **12.6%** | **21.4%** |
| GPT-5 high + code | 10.6% | 20.0% |
| Gemini 2.5 Pro | 2.0% | 8.1% |
| o3 (high) | 1.4% | 7.4% |
| DeepSeek-R1 | 1.1% | 5.1% |
| GPT-4o | 0.0% | 1.7% |

**Current leaderboard (April 2026, Artificial Analysis):**

| Model | Score |
|---|---|
| **GPT-5.4 Pro (xhigh)** | **30.0%** |
| Gemini 3 Deep Think | 25.7% |
| GPT-5.4 (xhigh) | 23.4% |

Even with tool access, the best model at publication (GPT-5) reached only 12.6% on full research challenges, while GPT-4o scored 0.0%. By April 2026, GPT-5.4 Pro reaches 30.0% — meaningful progress, but well below expert level.

## FoxBrain Relevance
CritPt's Condensed Matter Physics and Quantum Information domains are directly relevant to Foxconn's semiconductor and materials science R&D divisions. The benchmark's zero-contamination guarantee (all problems from unpublished active research) makes it one of the most reliable measures of genuine physics reasoning ability — critical before deploying FoxBrain as a research assistant in Foxconn's advanced materials or chip design groups. The checkpoint decomposition structure provides a diagnostic framework: FoxBrain can be evaluated step-by-step on multi-stage physics derivations, identifying exactly where reasoning breaks down before full autonomous research deployment.
