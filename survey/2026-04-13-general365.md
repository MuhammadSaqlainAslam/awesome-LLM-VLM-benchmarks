# General365: A K-12 General Reasoning Benchmark with Structural Variants (2026)

## Problem
Existing reasoning benchmarks either require specialist expertise (GPQA, FrontierScience) or test narrow skills (GSM8K for arithmetic, ARC for science). There is a gap at the "general intelligence" level — problems requiring only common K-12 knowledge but high reasoning capability: complex constraint satisfaction, nested logical branches, and semantic interference from misleading surface-level cues. This level represents the reasoning capability most relevant to general-purpose enterprise AI assistants, but no benchmark isolated it with controlled structural variants that reveal whether models generalise or overfit to surface patterns.

## Method
**General365** (arXiv: 2604.11778, April 13, 2026) constructs **1,460 problems** — **365 seed problems** each extended into **3 structural variants** (1,095 variants total) — across 8 reasoning categories. Variants systematically modify surface presentation (wording, ordering, distractors) while preserving logical structure, enabling precise measurement of whether performance drops are due to genuine reasoning limitations vs. pattern matching to problem form.

**8 reasoning categories:**
1. Arithmetic & Numerical Reasoning
2. Logical Deduction
3. Constraint Satisfaction
4. Nested Conditional Branching
5. Semantic Interference (misleading surface cues)
6. Spatial Reasoning
7. Temporal Ordering
8. Analogical Reasoning

Authors: Junlin Liu, Shengnan An, Shuang Zhou, Dan Ma.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **1,460 problems:** 365 seeds + 1,095 structural variants (3 per seed)
- **8 reasoning categories** spanning K-12-level general knowledge
- **No specialist expertise required** — tests pure reasoning capability
- **Structural variants** enable surface-vs-logic performance decomposition
- **Metric:** Accuracy; performance delta between seed and variants (generalisation gap)

## Key Results

| Model | Best Accuracy | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Best model** | **62.8%** | Model name not disclosed |
| Average frontier model | Below 62.8% | Significant headroom |
| Human baseline | ~90%+ | K-12 knowledge accessible to most adults |

- **Best frontier model achieves only 62.8%** on K-12-level general reasoning — a significant gap below human performance on problems not requiring specialist knowledge
- **Performance degrades substantially on structural variants** of the same logical problem, confirming that models are partially pattern-matching to problem form rather than reasoning through logic
- Semantic interference (misleading surface cues) is the hardest category — models are consistently tripped by irrelevant but plausible-sounding information
- Nested conditional branching is the second hardest — performance degrades sharply beyond 3 levels of nesting

## FoxBrain Relevance
General365 defines the baseline general reasoning capability required for FoxBrain's everyday enterprise assistant functions — drafting, planning, decision support, and problem-solving tasks that require no specialist knowledge but do require sound logical inference. The 62.8% SOTA on K-12 problems reveals a critical gap: FoxBrain may fail on apparently simple logical reasoning tasks that any trained employee would handle correctly. The structural variant methodology provides a contamination-resistant evaluation technique for FoxBrain's ongoing capability monitoring — generate variants of existing test problems to verify generalisation rather than memorisation. Priority domains: constraint satisfaction (supply chain planning), nested conditional branching (process decision trees), and semantic interference (compliance documents with misleading formatting).
