# Paper Overview: DeepSeek-V3 Technical Report

## Problem
What problem does this paper solve? (1–2 sentences)
Training and serving dense models with hundreds of billions of parameters is computationally prohibitive. DeepSeek-V3 aims to achieve "frontier-level" performance (GPT-4o class) while significantly reducing training costs and inference latency through architectural innovations like Multi-head Latent Attention (MLA).

## Method
What approach did they use? (1–2 sentences)
The model utilizes a **Mixture-of-Experts (MoE)** architecture with 671B total parameters (37B activated per token) and pioneers an **auxiliary-loss-free load balancing** strategy to prevent performance degradation. It was pre-trained on 14.8 trillion tokens using an FP8 mixed-precision framework to maximize hardware efficiency.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
List all benchmarks and datasets used.
* **Knowledge:** MMLU, MMLU-Pro, GPQA Diamond, SimpleQA.
* **Code & Math:** LiveCodeBench (Aug-Nov 2024), HumanEval-Mul, MATH-500, AIME 2024.
* **Engineering:** SWE-bench Verified, Aider-Edit.
* **Open-ended:** Arena-Hard, AlpacaEval 2.0.

## Key Results
At least one concrete number.
DeepSeek-V3 achieved **75.9% on MMLU-Pro** and **42.0% on SWE-bench Verified**, surpassing all other open-source models at the time and narrowing the gap with closed-source models like Claude 3.5 Sonnet. Remarkably, it was trained using only **2.788M H800 GPU hours**.

## FoxBrain Relevance
Is there anything we can learn or apply to FoxBrain?
Highly relevant for cost-efficiency. DeepSeek-V3 proves that we do not need infinite compute to reach SOTA levels if we optimize our architecture (MLA/MoE) and training precision (FP8). We should investigate their **auxiliary-loss-free load balancing** to improve our MoE expert utilization without sacrificing accuracy.
