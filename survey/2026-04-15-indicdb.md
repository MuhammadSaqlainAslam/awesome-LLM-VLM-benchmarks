# IndicDB: Benchmarking Multilingual Text-to-SQL Capabilities in Indian Languages (2026)

## Problem
Text-to-SQL benchmarks have overwhelmingly focused on English, with no large-scale, high-quality evaluation covering Indian languages despite their representation of over 1.5 billion speakers and growing enterprise database usage in South Asian markets. Existing multilingual datasets either use translated academic schemas (non-representative of real administrative data) or lack the structural complexity needed to evaluate cross-lingual semantic parsing in real government and enterprise settings.

## Method
**IndicDB** (arXiv: 2604.13686, April 15, 2026) constructs a multilingual Text-to-SQL benchmark using realistic administrative data from Indian government platforms, converted from denormalized formats into structured relational schemas via a three-agent framework. The benchmark generates 15,617 tasks across English, Hindi, and five additional Indic languages from 20 databases with 237 tables, featuring high relational complexity (11.85 tables per database, join depths up to 6 levels).

Authors: Aviral Dawar, Roshan Karanth, Vikram Goyal, Dhruv Kumar

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- 20 databases across 237 tables sourced from Indian government platforms
- 15,617 total tasks across 7 languages (English + Hindi + 5 Indic languages)
- Average 11.85 tables per database with join depths reaching 6 levels
- Models tested: DeepSeek v3.2, MiniMax 2.7, LLaMA 3.3, Qwen3
- Three-agent construction framework for schema normalization and task generation

## Key Results

| Language Group | Text-to-SQL Performance | Performance Gap |
|---|---|---|
| English | Highest | Baseline |
| Indic Languages (avg) | −9.00% vs. English | "Indic Gap" |
| Hindi (best Indic) | Closest to English | Smaller gap |

- A consistent 9.00% performance drop from English to Indic languages is identified as the "Indic Gap," stemming from schema linking difficulties, structural ambiguity, and limited external knowledge in Indic languages.
- All four tested models (DeepSeek v3.2, MiniMax 2.7, LLaMA 3.3, Qwen3) exhibit the Indic Gap, confirming this is a systematic limitation rather than model-specific.
- High relational complexity (join depth 6, 11.85 tables/DB) reveals that cross-lingual semantic parsing degrades fastest on multi-join queries, which are exactly the patterns most common in real administrative databases.

## FoxBrain Relevance
IndicDB directly applies to Foxconn's Indian manufacturing operations at facilities in Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, and Karnataka, where enterprise database queries and ERP interactions must work across English and multiple regional languages for local staff. FoxBrain's text-to-SQL capabilities powering supply chain analytics and production reporting dashboards need to be certified against the Indic Gap before deployment in Indian plants. The benchmark's use of actual government administrative schemas (similar to compliance reporting databases) makes it highly relevant for Foxconn's regulatory filing and audit workflows in India. The three-agent construction methodology provides a replicable pipeline for generating FoxBrain-specific multilingual database test suites from Foxconn's own ERP schema exports.
