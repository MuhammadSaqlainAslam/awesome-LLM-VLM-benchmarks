# ROVER: Recursive Reasoning Over Videos for Embodied Tasks (2025)

## Problem
Vision-language models excel at single-image understanding but fail when reasoning over **extended sequences of camera frames** in long-horizon video trajectories. This limits their utility for embodied agents that must track task progress, recognize failure states, and answer questions about ongoing manipulation tasks.

## Method
ROVER (Recursive Reasoning Over VidEo Recursively) introduces a **recursive decomposition framework** that segments long video trajectories into shorter subtask segments. At each recursion step, the model reasons over a small window of frames corresponding to a coherent subtask, then aggregates these local inferences into a global understanding of the full trajectory. This approach:
- Reduces the number of frames processed per step, mitigating hallucinations.
- Scales **linearly with video length** (vs. quadratic for full-sequence attention).
- Handles unexpected or non-optimal moments in the trajectory more robustly.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **OpenX Embodiment Videos:** Diverse real-world robot manipulation trajectories.
- **RoboCasa Dataset:** 543 videos across 27 robotic manipulation tasks (new dataset introduced in this work).
- **Tasks Evaluated:**
  1. Task progress estimation
  2. Frame-level natural language reasoning
  3. Video question answering (VideoQA)
- **Metrics:** Task success %, reasoning accuracy, VQA accuracy.

## Key Results
ROVER outperforms strong full-sequence baselines across all three video reasoning tasks on both OpenX and RoboCasa. Recursive decomposition reduces hallucination rates on non-optimal trajectory moments and maintains strong performance on complex 27-task manipulation scenarios where monolithic VLMs degrade significantly.

## FoxBrain Relevance
Directly applicable to FoxBrain's **manufacturing inspection and assembly monitoring** scenarios. Foxconn production lines generate long video sequences of repetitive tasks; ROVER's recursive approach can identify defect-insertion frames, track assembly progress, and answer QA queries over multi-minute inspection videos without quadratic compute cost.
