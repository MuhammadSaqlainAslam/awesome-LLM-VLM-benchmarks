# AVID: A Benchmark for Omni-Modal Audio-Visual Inconsistency Understanding via Agent-Driven Construction (2026)

## Problem
Existing multimodal benchmarks focus primarily on visual-text alignment and fail to evaluate models on fine-grained audio-visual inconsistencies within long videos — a critical capability for detecting manipulated or fabricated content. No prior benchmark provided large-scale, annotated data covering the full taxonomy of audio-visual conflict types (e.g., temporal desynchronization, semantic mismatch, acoustic-scene misalignment), making it impossible to systematically measure model competence in this domain.

## Method
**AVID** (arXiv: 2604.13593, April 15, 2026) introduces a large-scale benchmark featuring 11,200 long videos (average duration 235.5 seconds) with 39,400 annotated inconsistency events across 8 fine-grained categories. The benchmark uses an agent-driven construction pipeline with temporal segmentation, strategy planning, and specialized injectors to synthetically introduce controlled conflict scenarios. It evaluates models on 4 tasks: inconsistency detection, temporal grounding, classification, and holistic reasoning.

Authors: Zixuan Chen, Depeng Wang, Hao Lin, Li Luo, Ke Xu, Ya Guo, Huijia Zhu, Tanfeng Sun, Xinghao Jiang

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- 11,200 long videos, average duration 235.5 seconds
- 39,400 annotated audio-visual inconsistency events
- 78,700 segment clips extracted and labeled
- 8 fine-grained inconsistency categories
- 4 evaluation tasks: detection, temporal grounding, classification, reasoning
- Baseline model: AVID-Qwen (fine-tuned on benchmark data)

## Key Results

| Model | BLEU-4 (Reasoning) | Temporal Grounding mIoU | SODA-m Score |
|---|---|---|---|
| AVID-Qwen (baseline) | 2.8× improvement | 36.1% | 7.47 |
| Alternative models | Lower | 26.2% | 6.15 |

- AVID-Qwen achieves 2.8× higher BLEU-4 in segment reasoning compared to base models without fine-tuning.
- Temporal grounding mIoU improves from 26.2% (alternatives) to 36.1% (AVID-Qwen), highlighting the value of domain-specific training.
- Holistic understanding scores (SODA-m) of 7.47 vs. 6.15 confirm that integrated reasoning across modalities remains a hard open problem for current omni-modal LLMs.

## FoxBrain Relevance
AVID's audio-visual inconsistency detection capabilities map directly to Foxconn's quality assurance use cases, where manufacturing process videos must be verified for synchronization between audio alerts (alarm tones, machine beeps) and visual events (equipment faults, assembly defects). FoxBrain deployed on factory floor inspection systems could leverage AVID-style evaluation to certify its ability to detect sensor-camera desynchronization and flag unreliable telemetry. The 8-category inconsistency taxonomy provides a blueprint for annotating Foxconn's own multi-sensor industrial video datasets. The benchmark's agent-driven construction pipeline is replicable for generating synthetic fault injection training data without manual annotation overhead.
