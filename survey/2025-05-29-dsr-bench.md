# DSR-Bench: Evaluating Structural Reasoning Abilities of LLMs via Data Structures (2025)

## Problem
LLMs are widely used for tasks involving order, hierarchy, and connectivity, yet there is no principled benchmark that rigorously isolates structural reasoning ability. Standard benchmarks conflate this with language understanding, making it impossible to determine whether failures are due to reasoning deficits or language encoding issues.

## Method
DSR-Bench uses data structures as a controlled lens to evaluate structural reasoning — specifically the ability to understand and manipulate relationships including order, hierarchy, and connectivity. An automated generation and evaluation pipeline creates 4,140 problem instances spanning 20 data structures (arrays, trees, graphs, red-black trees, hash maps, etc.), 35 operations, and 3 difficulty levels, with two variants: DSR-Bench-spatial (diagram-based) and DSR-Bench-natural (natural language). 13 LLMs are evaluated.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **DSR-Bench:** 4,140 instances; 20 data structures; 35 operations; 3 difficulty levels.
- **Variants:** DSR-Bench-spatial (visual/diagram), DSR-Bench-natural (NL encoding).
- **Metrics:** Accuracy per data structure, per operation type; overall structural reasoning score (0–1).
- **GitHub:** https://github.com/TencentARC/DSR_Suite

## Key Results
The top-performing model achieves only **0.46/1.0** on challenging instances — barely above random on the hardest structural reasoning tasks. Performance degrades consistently as operation complexity and data structure depth increase, with tree and graph operations showing the largest gaps between frontier and smaller models.

## FoxBrain Relevance
High relevance for FoxBrain's supply chain, process graph, and hierarchical knowledge base applications. If FoxBrain is expected to reason over bill-of-materials trees, dependency graphs, or ordered manufacturing sequences, DSR-Bench provides the exact diagnostic needed. The 0.46 ceiling on challenging instances signals that structural reasoning must be explicitly addressed in FoxBrain's training or tool-use pipeline.
