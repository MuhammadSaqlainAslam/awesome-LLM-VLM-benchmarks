# ERNIE 5.0 Technical Report (2026)

## Problem
Existing frontier models treat text and vision as separate modalities stitched together via adapters, or require separate expert models per modality. This architectural fragmentation limits cross-modal synergy, wastes parameters, and prevents unified scaling across text, image, video, and audio in a single coherent model.

## Method
ERNIE 5.0 is Baidu's **natively autoregressive, trillion-parameter unified multimodal foundation model**, built on three core innovations:

1. **Ultra-Sparse MoE Architecture:** Activation rate below **3%** — allows massive capacity expansion without proportional compute cost. Uses **modality-agnostic expert routing**, meaning the same experts serve text, image, video, and audio tokens.
2. **Unified Next-Group-of-Tokens Prediction:** All modalities trained from scratch under a single objective, treating multimodal sequences as interleaved token groups rather than separate streams.
3. **Elastic Training Paradigm:** Within a single pre-training run, the model learns a **family of sub-models** with varying depths, expert capacities, and routing sparsity — enabling deployment at multiple operating points (cost vs. quality) without re-training.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **Scale:** Trillion-parameter MoE — first production-scale unified autoregressive model at this size supporting both multimodal understanding and generation.
- **Benchmarks reported:** Standard multimodal understanding benchmarks (MMMU, MMBench, etc.) and generation quality evaluations across image, video, and audio.
- **Modalities:** Text, image, video, audio — all trained jointly from scratch.

## Key Results
ERNIE 5.0 is the first publicly disclosed production-scale realization of a **unified trillion-parameter multimodal autoregressive model** that handles understanding and generation in a single architecture. The elastic training paradigm enables deploying a single checkpoint at multiple cost-quality operating points — a significant practical advantage over training separate models per deployment tier.

## FoxBrain Relevance
ERNIE 5.0's modality-agnostic MoE routing and elastic training paradigm are directly relevant to FoxBrain's **long-term architecture roadmap**. The <3% activation rate demonstrates that trillion-scale multimodal capability is achievable at manageable inference cost — a key consideration for Foxconn's on-premise deployment requirements. The elastic sub-model family also maps well to FoxBrain's need for both fast edge-inference variants and high-accuracy central models.
