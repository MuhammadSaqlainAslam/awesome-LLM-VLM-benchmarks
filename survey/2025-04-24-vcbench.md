# VCBench: Benchmarking Multimodal Mathematical Reasoning with Explicit Visual Dependency (2025)

## Problem
Most multimodal math benchmarks allow models to solve problems using text alone or with a single reference image, masking fundamental failures in visual dependency reasoning. No existing benchmark specifically targets problems where the answer cannot be derived without actively integrating information across multiple images in a defined visual context.

## Method
VCBench curates 1,720 elementary-level math problems (grades 1–6) each requiring reasoning across an average of 3.9 images (6,697 total). Problems span six cognitive domains — Time/Calendar, Spatial/Positional, Geometry/Shapes, Objects/Motion, Reasoning/Observation, and Organization/Patterns — and five competency levels. 26 leading large vision-language models (LVLMs) are evaluated with careful controls to prevent text-only solving.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **VCBench:** 1,720 problems; 6,697 images; 6 cognitive domains; 5 competency levels.
- **Models Evaluated:** 26 LVLMs including GPT-4o, Gemini, Claude, Qwen-VL, InternVL families.
- **Metrics:** Accuracy per domain, per competency level; overall accuracy.
- **GitHub:** https://github.com/alibaba-damo-academy/VCBench

## Key Results
Even top-performing models among the 26 evaluated fail to exceed **50% accuracy**, despite the problems being elementary-school level. Models show consistent failure on temporal and spatial domains, indicating that multi-image visual dependency remains a fundamental unsolved challenge for current LVLMs.

## FoxBrain Relevance
Exposes a critical gap for any FoxBrain visual reasoning pipeline operating on multi-panel diagrams, assembly sequences, or time-series imagery. The six VCBench cognitive domains map directly onto industrial visual QA scenarios (spatial layout understanding, temporal process tracking). FoxBrain should be evaluated on VCBench before any deployment involving multi-image visual reasoning tasks.
