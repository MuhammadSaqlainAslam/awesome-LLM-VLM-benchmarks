# IFBench: Generalizing Verifiable Instruction Following (2025)

## Problem
Existing instruction-following benchmarks — primarily IFEval with its 25 constraint templates — are too narrow. Models achieve 80%+ on IFEval by overfitting to a small, fixed set of constraints, yet fail dramatically on **novel, out-of-distribution output constraints** they have not seen during training. There is no benchmark testing whether models can genuinely generalise precise instruction-following to unseen constraint types.

## Method
IFBench introduces **58 new verifiable output constraints** — all out-of-domain and distinct from IFEval's 25 — organised into 7 categories: **count, ratio, words, sentence, format, custom, and copy**. Evaluation uses 300 diverse prompts drawn from WildChat (real user interactions), combined with the new constraints. All constraints are programmatically verifiable, enabling scalable automatic evaluation. The paper also releases 29 training constraints with verification functions and RLVR (Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards) training prompts. IF-RLVR training is demonstrated to dramatically improve generalisation to unseen constraints without sacrificing performance on seen ones. Published at **NeurIPS 2025 Datasets and Benchmarks Track** by AI2 and University of Washington.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **58 new verifiable out-of-domain constraints** (vs. IFEval's 25)
- **7 constraint categories:** count, ratio, words, sentence, format, custom, copy
- **300 WildChat prompts** for evaluation
- **IFEval (25 constraints)** used as in-distribution comparison to quantify overfitting
- Also released: 29 training constraints + verification functions + RLVR training data

## Key Results

| Model | IFEval Score | IFBench Score |
|---|---|---|
| GPT-4 / Claude 3.7 Sonnet | >80% | **<50%** |
| Qwen3-32B | — | <50% |
| Tulu-3-8B (baseline SFT) | 82.4% | 28.9% |
| Tulu-3-8B + IF-RLVR | 92.2% | **45.9%** (+17pp) |
| **Qwen 2.5 7B + IF-RLVR** | 87.8% | **54.7%** |

Frontier models scoring >80% on IFEval collapse to **below 50% on IFBench** — exposing that high IFEval scores reflect overfitting, not true generalisation. IF-RLVR training on verifiable constraints improves IFBench score by +17pp (Tulu-3-8B) to +25pp (Qwen 2.5 7B) while also improving IFEval performance, demonstrating that verifiable reward training genuinely improves constraint-following generalisation.

## FoxBrain Relevance
IFBench directly addresses one of FoxBrain's most critical production risks: structured output reliability. Foxconn's downstream automation pipelines depend on FoxBrain adhering precisely to output format constraints (JSON schema, field length limits, ratio specifications) — exactly the constraint types IFBench tests. The finding that frontier models collapse from >80% to <50% on novel constraints means FoxBrain's IFEval score should be treated as a floor, not a ceiling. IF-RLVR training on Foxconn-specific output constraints (report formats, BOM templates, QC log schemas) is a direct, low-cost path to dramatically improving FoxBrain's production reliability.
