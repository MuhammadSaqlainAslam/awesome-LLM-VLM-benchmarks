# LiveMathematicianBench: A Dynamic Benchmark for Evaluating Mathematical Theorem Proving in LLMs (2026)

## Problem
Static math benchmarks (MATH, AIME, OlympiadBench) suffer from training-data contamination — frontier models can memorise solutions rather than demonstrate genuine mathematical reasoning. Existing benchmarks also lack coverage of graduate-level theorem-proving and cannot distinguish models that truly understand proofs from those that pattern-match to memorised answers. There was no continuously refreshed, contamination-resistant benchmark specifically targeting university-level mathematical theorem proving.

## Method
LiveMathematicianBench constructs a **dynamic MCQ benchmark** drawing problems from recent arXiv mathematics preprints (submitted within 12 months of evaluation), ensuring no paper appears in any model's training data. A **13-category theorem taxonomy** classifies each problem: Algebra, Analysis, Combinatorics, Differential Geometry, Graph Theory, Linear Algebra, Number Theory, Optimization, Probability, Set Theory, Statistics, Topology, and Applied Mathematics. **Proof-sketch-guided distractors** are generated by a pipeline that extracts intermediate proof steps and constructs plausible but incorrect alternative conclusions, creating MCQ options that require genuine understanding rather than surface recognition. A **substitution-resistant evaluation** mode replaces variable names, constants, and symbolic labels with equivalents to test generalization beyond symbol memorisation. Published April 2, 2026 by Linyang He, Qiyao Yu, Hanze Dong, Baohao Liao, Xinxing Xu, Micah Goldblum, Jiang Bian, and Nima Mesgarani (arXiv: 2604.01754).

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **Dynamic MCQ** sourced from arXiv papers (< 12 months old at evaluation time)
- **13 theorem categories:** Algebra, Analysis, Combinatorics, Differential Geometry, Graph Theory, Linear Algebra, Number Theory, Optimization, Probability, Set Theory, Statistics, Topology, Applied Mathematics
- **Proof-sketch-guided distractors** generated from intermediate proof steps
- **Substitution-resistant mode** replaces variable names/constants to block symbol memorisation
- Evaluation modes: Standard MCQ accuracy vs. Substitution-Resistant accuracy

## Key Results

**Standard accuracy (recent arXiv problems):**

| Model | Standard Acc | Substitution-Resistant Acc |
|---|---|---|
| **Gemini-3.1-pro-preview** | **43.5%** | **17.6%** |
| GPT-5.4 | ~38% | 30.6% |
| Claude 3.7 Sonnet | ~34% | ~14% |
| Llama 4 Maverick | ~29% | ~11% |

- **Dramatic gap between standard and substitution-resistant accuracy**: Gemini-3.1-pro-preview drops from 43.5% → 17.6% under substitution, revealing heavy reliance on symbol memorisation
- GPT-5.4 shows a smaller gap (substitution-resistant: 30.6%), indicating stronger genuine proof understanding
- All models perform near random chance on **Differential Geometry** and **Topology** under substitution-resistant conditions
- **Number Theory** and **Combinatorics** show the highest relative robustness to substitution

## FoxBrain Relevance
LiveMathematicianBench establishes the ceiling for FoxBrain's graduate-level mathematical reasoning — directly relevant to R&D engineering, materials science calculations, and process optimization problems encountered at Foxconn. The substitution-resistant mode provides a contamination-proof evaluation method for FoxBrain checkpoints trained on technical documents: a pass on substitution-resistant theorem problems confirms genuine algebraic and optimization reasoning capability, not memorisation. The 13-category taxonomy maps onto FoxBrain's priority engineering domains: Optimization (yield/capacity), Graph Theory (supply chain networks), Statistics (quality control), and Linear Algebra (simulation workloads).
