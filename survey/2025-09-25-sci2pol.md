# Sci2Pol: Evaluating and Fine-tuning LLMs on Scientific-to-Policy Brief Generation (2025)

## Problem
Translating scientific findings into accessible policy briefs is a critical but understudied NLP task. No prior benchmark or training corpus existed to evaluate whether LLMs can reliably perform this multi-stage translation — from comprehension and summarization through structured generation and factual verification.

## Method
Sci2Pol introduces both a benchmark and a training corpus. The benchmark organizes 18 tasks around a five-stage taxonomy mirroring the human policy-writing process: Autocompletion, Understanding, Summarization, Generation, and Verification. The Sci2Pol-Corpus contains 639 high-quality paper-brief pairs curated from 5.6M policy documents. An LLM-as-judge metric evaluates generation quality. A fine-tuned Gemma-27B model is shown to outperform GPT-4o and DeepSeek-V3 (671B) on generation tasks.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **Sci2Pol Benchmark:** 18 tasks (multiple-choice and open-ended) across 5 stages.
- **Sci2Pol-Corpus:** 639 paper-brief pairs (training); curated from 5.6M policy documents.
- **Stages:** Autocompletion → Understanding → Summarization → Generation → Verification.
- **Metrics:** LLM-as-judge quality score; task accuracy per stage.

## Key Results
Fine-tuned **Gemma-27B** outperforms **GPT-4o** and **DeepSeek-V3 (671B)** on generation quality — demonstrating that task-specific fine-tuning on 639 high-quality examples dramatically outweighs raw scale. All baseline LLMs perform weakest on the Verification stage, confirming factual grounding as the key bottleneck.

## FoxBrain Relevance
Relevant for FoxBrain's government/enterprise reporting use cases. If FoxBrain is expected to produce regulatory briefs, ESG reports, or technical summaries for non-expert stakeholders, the Sci2Pol five-stage taxonomy provides a ready evaluation framework. The finding that fine-tuning 639 curated examples beats 671B parameters is a strong signal for FoxBrain's domain adaptation strategy.
