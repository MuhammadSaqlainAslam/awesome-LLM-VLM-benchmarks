# Paper Overview: Claude 4.6 Opus Technical Report

## Problem
Standard frontier models struggle with **"Action Fatigue"**—the tendency for agentic performance to degrade over long-horizon tasks (10+ steps). Claude 4.6 is designed to transition from simple tool-calling to sustained, multi-agent coordination.

## Method
Introduces **Adaptive Thinking** and **Agent Teams**. The model uses a dynamic reasoning budget (Low/Med/High/Max) to self-correct during long-running tasks. A new **Conversation Compaction** feature automatically summarizes historical context to prevent the performance "cliff" typically seen at the end of a 1M token window.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
* **GPQA Diamond:** **95.4%** (New SOTA).
* **ARC-AGI-2:** **68.8%** (Highest score for a standard commercial model).
* **Humanity's Last Exam (HLE):** **46.2%** (Exceeding Gemini 3.1 Pro).

## FoxBrain Relevance
The **Agent Teams** architecture (parallel Claude instances) is the blueprint for our internal "Manager-Worker" workflows. We should adopt the **Max Effort** setting specifically for codebase-wide refactoring tasks.
