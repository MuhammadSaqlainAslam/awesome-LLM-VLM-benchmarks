## Problem
Vision-Language Models often lack a "Composition of the World" (CoW), leading to physical hallucinations in videos and failure to understand spatial occlusions.

## Method
**CoW-Bench** evaluates VLMs on multi-frame video sequences where objects move, hide, or interact, testing if the model maintains a consistent "mental map" of the scene.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **Benchmark:** CoW-Bench
- **Dataset:** Synthetic and real-world multi-frame videos (physics-heavy)
- **Metrics:** Temporal Consistency Score, Spatial Reasoning Accuracy

## Key Results
Most current VLMs fail to track occluded objects in **55% of cases**, losing the "object identity" once it moves behind another element in a video sequence.

## FoxBrain Relevance
Critical if FoxBrain expands into video analysis or security monitoring. It sets the standard for how we measure "world-model" understanding in our vision pipeline.
