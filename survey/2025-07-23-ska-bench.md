# SKA-Bench: A Fine-Grained Benchmark for Structured Knowledge Understanding of LLMs (2025)

## Problem
LLMs are increasingly used to reason over structured knowledge sources — knowledge graphs, tables, and hybrid KG+text or table+text combinations — yet no benchmark provides fine-grained diagnostics across these four distinct structured knowledge forms simultaneously. Existing benchmarks conflate performance on clean inputs with robustness to noise and ordering variation.

## Method
SKA-Bench is a Structured Knowledge Augmented QA benchmark covering four structured knowledge forms: Knowledge Graphs (KG), Tables, KG+Text, and Table+Text. A three-stage pipeline constructs instances containing questions, answers, positive knowledge units, and noisy knowledge units. Each instance is then expanded into four fine-grained testbeds measuring: Noise Robustness, Order Insensitivity, Information Integration, and Negative Rejection. Published in EMNLP 2025 Findings.

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **SKA-Bench:** 921 SKA-QA base instances × 4 ability testbeds = 3,684 evaluation settings.
- **Knowledge Forms:** KG, Table, KG+Text, Table+Text.
- **Abilities:** Noise Robustness, Order Insensitivity, Information Integration, Negative Rejection.
- **Models Evaluated:** 8 LLMs including DeepSeek-R1.
- **GitHub:** https://github.com/zjukg/SKA-Bench

## Key Results
DeepSeek-R1 leads overall but shows surprising fragility on Order Insensitivity tasks — performance drops significantly when relevant knowledge units are not presented first. Negative Rejection (refusing to answer when no supporting evidence exists) is the hardest ability across all models and knowledge forms.

## FoxBrain Relevance
Directly applicable to FoxBrain's enterprise knowledge graph and structured database integration. The four SKA-Bench ability testbeds map cleanly onto real deployment risks: noise from imperfect KG extraction, sensitivity to data ordering in retrieval pipelines, multi-hop integration across KG+text, and hallucination when the knowledge base has no supporting evidence (Negative Rejection).
