# DR³-Eval: Towards Realistic and Reproducible Deep Research Evaluation (2026)

## Problem
Existing evaluation frameworks for Deep Research Agents (DRAs) lack realism — they use synthetic or static corpora that don't reflect real user research needs — and reproducibility, as web-based retrieval varies across runs. There is no standardized multi-dimensional evaluation methodology that measures the full range of capabilities required for deep research (planning, retrieval, multimodal understanding, and structured report generation).

## Method
**DR³-Eval** (arXiv: 2604.14683, April 16, 2026) introduces a benchmark featuring authentic user-provided materials paired with per-task static research sandbox corpora to ensure reproducibility. Each task includes supportive documents, distractor content, and noise to simulate real-world complexity. A five-dimensional evaluation framework measures Information Recall, Factual Accuracy, Citation Coverage, Instruction Following, and Depth Quality — all validated against human judgment.

Authors: Qianqian Xie and 18 co-authors

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- Authentic user-provided research materials as task inputs
- Per-task static sandbox corpora (supportive documents + distractors + noise)
- Five evaluation dimensions: Information Recall, Factual Accuracy, Citation Coverage, Instruction Following, Depth Quality
- Validated against human judgment
- Code and data publicly available

## Key Results

| Evaluation Dimension | Key Finding | Failure Mode |
|---|---|---|
| Information Recall | Partial success | Retrieval robustness failures with distractors |
| Factual Accuracy | Critical failures | Hallucination control breakdown on complex tasks |
| Citation Coverage | Variable | Source attribution degrades with noise |
| Instruction Following | Moderate | Multi-constraint instruction gaps |
| Depth Quality | Low on hard tasks | Shallow synthesis under complexity |

- **DR³-Agent testing reveals critical failure modes in retrieval robustness and hallucination control**
- The five-dimensional framework exposes independent failure modes invisible to single-metric evaluation
- Static sandbox corpora solve the reproducibility problem while maintaining realistic complexity

## FoxBrain Relevance
DR³-Eval's framework maps directly onto FoxBrain's enterprise deep research use cases — technical due diligence for supplier qualification, competitive intelligence reports, regulatory compliance research, and R&D literature synthesis. The benchmark's emphasis on retrieval robustness under distractor documents parallels Foxconn's internal knowledge bases where relevant specifications coexist with outdated revisions and unrelated documents. The five-dimensional evaluation (especially Citation Coverage and Depth Quality) provides a principled quality gate for FoxBrain's research report generation capabilities before deployment in Foxconn's procurement and engineering teams.
