# SAGE: A Service Agent Graph-Guided Evaluation Benchmark (2026)

## Problem
Customer service agents represent one of the highest-volume enterprise LLM deployments, yet existing agent benchmarks use generic task-completion metrics that fail to capture the critical requirements of real service contexts: logical compliance with business process rules, coverage of all required dialogue paths, and emotional resilience under adversarial customer pressure. Models that pass generic task benchmarks frequently fail in production customer service because they skip required verification steps, miss legally mandated disclosures, or capitulate to emotionally aggressive customers. No benchmark systematically evaluated all three of these service-critical dimensions simultaneously.

## Method
**SAGE** (arXiv: 2604.09285, April 10, 2026) evaluates **27 LLMs** across **6 real industrial customer service scenarios** using **Dynamic Dialogue Graphs (DDGs)** — directed graphs that encode all valid service dialogue paths, required decision points, and mandatory disclosures for each scenario type. The benchmark introduces a **dual-axis evaluation framework:**

1. **Logical Compliance Axis:**
   - *Path Coverage Score* — fraction of required dialogue nodes the agent covers
   - *Intent Compliance Rate* — whether derived actions match the classified customer intent
   - Identifies the **"Execution Gap"**: models correctly classify intent but then execute the wrong derived action

2. **Empathy Resilience Axis:**
   - *Empathy Score under Adversarial Pressure* — measures politeness, de-escalation, and protocol adherence when customers are angry, distressed, or manipulative
   - Identifies **"Empathy Drift"**: models that maintain logical compliance under pressure but abandon empathetic tone

Authors: Ling Shi, Yuqin Dai, Ziyin Wang, Ning Gao et al. (10 total).

## Benchmarks / Datasets
- **6 real industrial customer service scenarios** (financial services, telecommunications, retail, insurance, logistics, healthcare)
- **27 LLMs evaluated**
- **Dynamic Dialogue Graphs (DDGs)** encoding all valid service paths, decision points, and required disclosures
- **Dual-axis evaluation:** Logical Compliance (Path Coverage + Intent Compliance) + Empathy Resilience
- **Metrics:** Path coverage score, intent compliance rate, empathy score under adversarial pressure

## Key Results

| Finding | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Execution Gap (universal)** | All 27 models correctly classify intent but execute wrong derived action — a universal failure mode |
| **Empathy Drift** | Models maintaining logical compliance often abandon empathetic tone under adversarial pressure |
| **Path Coverage** | Coverage decreases significantly as dialogue trees grow deeper; all models miss non-obvious required nodes |
| **Best models** | Closed-source frontier models lead on both axes but still exhibit the Execution Gap |

- **The Execution Gap is universal across all 27 evaluated LLMs:** correct intent classification does NOT guarantee correct derived action execution — a fundamental disconnect between semantic understanding and procedural compliance
- Intent classification accuracy is a misleading proxy for service agent quality; DDG path coverage is the operationally relevant metric
- Adversarial customer pressure degrades both logical compliance and empathy simultaneously in all tested models
- Healthcare and insurance scenarios (most complex DDGs) show the largest Execution Gaps

## FoxBrain Relevance
SAGE directly benchmarks FoxBrain's customer-facing service agent capabilities for Foxconn's B2B and B2C service operations: order management, supply chain inquiry handling, warranty claims, and technical support. The Execution Gap — the universal failure to correctly execute derived actions despite correct intent classification — is the most critical finding for FoxBrain's service deployment: intent classification accuracy (easily measured) will dramatically overestimate real-world service correctness. FoxBrain's service agent evaluation must use DDG path coverage as the primary metric. The dual-axis framework (logical + empathy) should be adopted directly for FoxBrain's service QA: a model that handles logistics correctly but rudely escalates customer complaints is not production-ready.
